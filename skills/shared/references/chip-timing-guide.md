# Chip Timing Guide — Fantasy WC 2026

Four chips are available once each per season. The order and timing of deployment
determines a large fraction of your final score. This guide encodes the principles
learned from in-season analysis.

---

## The Core Principle

> **Use captain chips (TC, DC) EARLY — in group stage or R32.**
> Do NOT save them for semi-finals or the Final.

**Why early is better:**

| Stage | Teams remaining | Typical game type | Goals per game |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Group MD1-MD3 | 48 | Strong vs. weak (e.g. Brazil vs. small nation) | High |
| R32 | 32 | Group winners vs. weaker qualifiers | Medium-high |
| R16 | 16 | Quality teams, but still some mismatches | Medium |
| QF | 8 | All elite — balanced | Medium-low |
| SF | 4 | Elite vs. elite — tight | Low |
| Final | 2 | Two best teams — tactical, tight | Very low |

Captain chips multiply your captain's actual score. Fewer goals = lower captain
score = lower multiplier value. Spending TC ×3 on a final where the captain
scores 2 minutes points = 6 pts. Spending TC ×3 in R32 where the captain scores
2 goals = (9 + 2) × 3 = 33 pts. **Early deployment wins.**

---

## Chip 1: 5 Substitutions

**What it does:** Converts 3 transfers in a single round to 5 transfers.

**When to use:** The **last restricted-transfer round before the unlimited R32 window**.

**Why this exact timing:**
- Once the unlimited pre-R32 window opens, you can make unlimited transfers for free.
  The chip adds zero value in an unlimited window.
- In restricted rounds (3/round), the chip gives +2 extra transfers — fix bench dead
  weight + weak starters in one go.
- Don't use it in an early group round when the unlimited window is far away;
  save until you need all 5 slots at once.

**Trigger condition:** You have ≥4 players to fix simultaneously (bench dead weight
from eliminated nations, injured starters, expensive underperformers).

---

## Chip 2: All Squad Points

**What it does:** All 15 players (starters + bench) score for one round.

**When to use:** **Group stage MD3** — the final group matchday.

**Why MD3 specifically:**
- MD3 is the ONLY round in the entire tournament where all 48 teams play their
  final group game simultaneously. This guarantees all 15 players have a game.
- From R32 onward, eliminated nations' bench players score 0. Your bench becomes
  dead weight unless you upgraded it.
- By the time MD3 arrives, use the 5 Subs chip earlier to upgrade bench players
  to 3M players from strong qualifying nations — then ASP gives you 15 scoring
  players instead of 11 + 4 zeros.

**If MD3 has already passed:** Use ASP in R32 (still 32 teams, better than
QF/SF/Final where bench coverage is increasingly limited).

**Bench quality prerequisite:** Never use ASP when bench is all 3M dead weight
from eliminated nations. The 5 Subs chip should fire BEFORE ASP.

---

## Chip 3: Triple Captain

**What it does:** Captain scores ×3 instead of ×2 for one round.

**When to use:** **R32 (first knockout round).**

**Why R32:**
- Group stage winners are drawn against weaker R32 qualifiers (3rd-place teams).
  Still a meaningful scoring mismatch → high goal probability.
- The captain's ×3 multiplier is worth the most when goals are likely.
- Your captain (ideally a top striker who is also their nation's penalty taker)
  will face a manageable first-knockout opponent.
- By R32, the unlimited pre-R32 window has let you rebuild your squad around the
  best available penalty-taking striker — exactly when TC fires.

**If R32 has already passed:** Use in R16. Avoid saving for SF/Final.

---

## Chip 4: Double Captains

**What it does:** Captain AND vice-captain BOTH score ×2 for one round.
Stacks with Triple Captain: if both TC and DC are active in the same round,
captain scores ×3 and vice-captain scores ×2.

**When to use:** **R16.** OR stack with TC at R32 if the game allows 2 chips per round.

**Why R16:**
- R16 still has some structural mismatches (group winners from stronger groups
  vs. qualifiers from weaker groups).
- By R16, your captain + vice-captain slots should both be occupied by premium
  attacking penalty takers, maximising the double-double return.
- If used independently of TC, DC in R16 and TC in R32 is the optimal split.

**Stacking rule:** The rules explicitly state DC "stacks with Triple Captain
(captain ×3, vice ×2)". If the game allows 2 chips in one round, use TC + DC
simultaneously at R32 for maximum season return:
→ Captain ×3, VC ×2 in the round where scoring is still high but opposition is weaker.

## Recommended Chip Deployment Order

| Priority | Chip | Target Stage | Notes |
|----------|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | **5 Substitutions** | Last restricted round (group) | Urgent — valueless once unlimited window opens |
| 2 | **All Squad Points** | Group MD3 (last group matchday) | All 48 teams play; bench upgraded via chip 1 |
| 3 | **Triple Captain** | R32 | Captain ×3 vs. weaker R32 opponents |
| 4 | **Double Captains** | R16 (or R32 stacked with TC) | Both captains doubled; or stacked for ×3/×2 in R32 |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Saving TC for the Final** — by then, the two best teams in the world are
   playing. Tight game, fewer goals, lower captain score × 3 = wasted chip.

2. **Using ASP before bench is upgraded** — 4 dead bench players scoring 0 makes
   ASP worthless. Always run 5 Subs chip first.

3. **Using 5 Subs in an early group round** — if you have 2 group rounds left,
   a chip now and a free unlimited window later wastes the chip's power.
   Wait until the last restricted round when you can fix all 5 problems at once.

4. **Spending DC on a late-stage round where you only have 1 good captain** —
   DC requires BOTH captain and VC to be premium attackers to extract value.
   Set up the VC slot carefully before deploying DC.

5. **Using TC when your captain is doubtful** — a ×3 multiplier on a player
   who plays 45 minutes scores 1 × 3 = 3 pts. Devastating waste. Only activate
   TC when your captain is 100% certain to play 60+ minutes.
